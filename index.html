Her Story

1. What drew you to pursue a career and degree in tech and how was your experience studying IT?

"What drew me to pursue a degree and career in technology was my curiosity about how technology will shape our world in the future. I was also interested in the wide range of career opportunities that the field of IT offers. My experience studying IT was both challenging and rewarding. My first year in college happened during the pandemic, so most of our classes were conducted online, which made the experience quite different from the usual college setup. Despite that, I still found opportunities to grow by joining our organization, EXPLICIT, and participating in different activities. In my final year, I became more active in joining hackathons, competitions and pageantry, which helped me gain valuable experiences."

2. What are the challenges you have faced as a woman in IT?

"One of the challenges of being a woman in IT is that the field is still often seen as male-dominated. Even now in my workplace, there are five Web Specialists on the team and I am the only woman, so there are moments where it can feel intimidating. However, these experiences helped shape me to become more confident in my skills and voice my ideas. Being involved in organizations and competitions during college also helped me prove to myself that I belong in this field. These experiences motivated me to keep improving my technical knowledge and communication skills, and instead of seeing it as a limitation, I see it as an opportunity to represent women in tech and inspire others to pursue the same path."

3. How did you balance work, studies, and competitions?

"Balancing it all at once was definitely challenging. I actually started working during my first year of college, and it helped me support myself, my family, and save money. In my final year, I decided to stop working so I could focus on my studies, our capstone project, internship, and fully enjoy my last year in college. Because of that decision, I was able to become more active in our organization, join hackathons and competitions, and even participate in pageantry—experiences I never imagined I would have. One important lesson I learned is that while it is possible to juggle many responsibilities, it is also important to rest and take care of yourself."

4. What is your message to other women in tech?

"My message is to believe in your potential and not be afraid to take space in this field. When I participated in the PUP pageant, my advocacy was Women Empowerment because I believe women have the ability to lead, innovate, and make meaningful contributions in fields like technology that are often seen as male-dominated. This belief is also reflected in my personal motto, 'I have the means.' It reminds me that I already possess the confidence, skills, and determination needed to grow and succeed. For me, it is a simple reminder that success is possible when you make the most of the opportunities and abilities you already have."
